# Cognitive Biases as Attack Surface

Cognitive biases are not the cause of faulty decisions but emergent effects of the context in which decisions are made. 
They reveal where the decision system is dominated by automatic heuristics and where controlled, reflective cognition recedes. 
The hacker does not use these effects to judge individuals but to identify vulnerable decision points.

### Conditions Under Which Biases Appear
Cognitive biases occur reliably when certain parameters in the decision context are met:

- **Time pressure:** Accelerates the use of heuristics such as *availability* and *anchoring*. 
- **Social environments:** Activate conformity and authority biases. 
- **Stress and information overload:** Reinforce simplifications and shortcut decisions. 
- **Assumptions and group affiliation:** Favor pattern recognition over analytical evaluation.

### Operational Principle
Biases are sensors, not levers. 
They indicate where context manipulations are effective, but the manipulation itself always occurs at the level of situational framing, not within individual cognitive mechanisms.

### Mapping the Biases
Each bias can be assigned to a parameter of the decision environment:

- **Availability Bias →** Emphasis on current or salient information 
- **Anchoring Bias →** Initial reference points in a scenario 
- **Confirmation Bias →** Selective presentation of evidence 
- **Authority Bias →** Explicit or implicit hierarchy cues 
- **Groupthink →** Peer alignment and normative pressure 

The attacker does not attack the bias directly. 
Instead, the following factors are deliberately adjusted:
- Timing 
- Information flow 
- Social signals 
- Cognitive load 

This creates a decision topology in which errors emerge autonomously.

### Key Insights
Cognitive biases are predictable effects, not manipulable traits. 
The attack surface is contextual, structural, and operational. 
Mapping the biases reveals *where* and *how* the context must be shaped — not *who* is being targeted.
